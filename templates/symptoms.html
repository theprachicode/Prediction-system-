Recognizing Symptoms

Early identification can prevent severe complications.

Tingling or Burning

Often described as a "pins and needles" sensation. Usually starts in the toes and feet and gradually spreads upward.

Numbness

A reduced ability to feel pain or temperature changes. You may not notice a blister or cut on your foot.

Sharp Pains

Sharp, jabbing, shooting, or cramp-like pain, often worsening at night and interrupting sleep.

Extreme Sensitivity

Hyperesthesia. For some, even the weight of a bedsheet can cause severe discomfort or pain.

Loss of Balance

Weakness in muscles, leading to changes in the way you walk, loss of coordination, and frequent falls.

Foot Ulcers

In severe cases, injuries go unnoticed due to numbness, leading to serious infections, ulcers, and bone pain.
