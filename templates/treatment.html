Treatment Options

Managing symptoms and slowing progression

Medications
  • Anti-seizure drugs: Gabapentin and pregabalin are commonly prescribed for nerve pain.
  • Antidepressants: SNRIs (Duloxetine) can interfere with chemical processes in the brain that cause pain.
  • Topical treatments: Capsaicin cream or lidocaine patches.
Therapies
  • Physical therapy: Helps maintain balance, muscle strength, and flexibility.
  • Spinal cord stimulation: Electrical signals block pain messages.
  • Acupuncture: May help relieve the pain of neuropathy over multiple sessions.
Complication Management
  • Wound Care: Aggressive treatment of foot ulcers to prevent amputation.
  • Orthotics: Custom shoes to relieve pressure points on the feet.
  • Surgery: To decompress nerves in specific cases (like carpal tunnel).
