Clinical Diagnosis

How medical professionals confirm DPN

Comprehensive Foot Exam

A doctor will check your skin for cracks or sores, as well as test your temperature, touch, and vibration senses. Tools used include a tuning fork and a monofilament (a soft nylon bristle) to assess sensory loss.

Did you know?

The American Diabetes Association recommends an annual comprehensive foot exam for all patients with diabetes.

Nerve Conduction Studies (NCS)

This test measures how quickly the nerves in your arms and legs conduct electrical signals. It's often used to diagnose carpal tunnel syndrome as well as neuropathy.

Electromyography (EMG)

Often performed along with NCS. It measures the electrical discharges produced in muscles. A very thin needle electrode is inserted into specific muscles to record activity.

Quantitative Sensory Testing (QST)

A noninvasive test used to assess how your nerves respond to vibration and changes in temperature.

Autonomic Testing

Tests may be done to determine how your blood pressure changes in different positions and if you sweat normally.
